"Argentina, Política en Crudo" - Documentary

Exploring Argentina's 2023 Presidential Elections Through Immigrant Eyes

This documentary takes a close look at Argentina's political landscape during the 2023 presidential elections through the eyes of its immigrant community. The film weaves together stories from four immigrants, adding unique layers to the narrative.

Embark on a compelling journey into the lives of Argentina's immigrant community during the 2023 presidential elections. The interviews unfold like a tapestry, unveiling the unique experiences of Divina Iñigues, Sonia Leite de Cunha, Riad Chamali, and Ian Lefram. Each narrative is a thread in the intricate mosaic—Divina reflecting on her childhood immigration, Sonia navigating hope and challenges, Riad sharing profound reflections, and Ian unveiling the struggles of fitting into a new country while holding onto hope for its future. This mosaic captures the diverse cultural landscape of Argentina, with a mix of ages, backgrounds, and aspirations.  The visual storytelling is equally impactful, with attention given to each edit, prop, and setting. The group discussion, where immigrants share their thoughts on the country's future, takes center stage. The preference for a socialist approach and the call for better education and security reveal shared concerns. The video editing enhances the emotional impact, highlighting the uncertainty and lack of trust in politics during this critical moment. The extensive research, varied interviews, and careful production details come together to create a compelling testament to the immigrant experience and their aspirations for Argentina's future. This documentary is a heartfelt project for me and my friends, all young adults and students, who collaborated on this deeply personal exploration of our hometown. We are all concerned about and actively working toward a better future for Argentina, making this film an emotionally involved journey for each one of us.
